Project Documentation: Stock Trading Training System

1. Project Mission and Scope
The Stock Trading Training System is a specialized software environment designed to simulate the complexities of a modern financial exchange. The primary mission of this project is to provide a risk-free sandbox where entry-level stockbrokers and retail customers can develop proficiency in market mechanics, order types, and portfolio management. By decoupling the trading experience from real-world capital, the system allows for the observation of market behaviors under various simulated conditions.

The scope of this project encompasses the development of a high-concurrency backend, a responsive web interface, and a deterministic yet random price fluctuation engine. The system is architected to handle multiple simultaneous users, ensuring that transactions are processed with ACID (Atomicity, Consistency, Isolation, Durability) compliance. This documentation serves as a comprehensive guide for developers, system administrators, and stakeholders to understand the underlying logic and deployment strategies of the platform.

2. Comprehensive Functional Requirements

2.1 Detailed Customer Operations
The customer experience is centered around the lifecycle of a trade and the management of liquid assets. Users must navigate several distinct modules to successfully interact with the simulated market.

Identity Management and Security:
Registration requires a multi-step validation process. Users must provide a full legal name for compliance tracking, a unique username for system identification, a verified email address for notifications, and a password that is hashed using industry-standard algorithms before being stored in the database.

Market Participation:
The system supports market orders, which are executed at the most recent price generated by the price engine. To reflect the reality of human error or changing market conditions, a cancellation window is implemented. This allows a user to retract a buy or sell request after submission but prior to the final ledger entry. Once the order passes the execution threshold, it is finalized and reflected in the user's history.

Asset and Liquidity Visualization:
The portfolio module provides a real-time aggregate of a user's financial health. It calculates the total value of stock holdings based on current market prices and adds this to the liquid cash balance to provide a total net worth.

Financial Ledger and Cash Movements:
The system treats the cash account as the primary hub for all liquidity. When a user deposits funds, the balance is updated in the relational database with a corresponding timestamp. Withdrawals are strictly governed by available funds; the system will reject any request that exceeds the current balance. Crucially, the proceeds from all stock sales are automatically routed to the cash account, ensuring that the user's buying power is updated in real-time.

2.2 Detailed Administrator Operations
The administrative layer provides the "Rules of Engagement" for the simulation, giving controllers the ability to manipulate the environment for specific training scenarios.

Asset Initialization:
Administrators are responsible for the genesis of all tradable entities. For each new stock, the admin must define the company name, a three or four-letter ticker symbol, the total volume of shares available to the public, and the initial listing price. This data serves as the baseline for the random price generator.

Temporal and Calendar Regulation:
Market stability is maintained through strict temporal controls. Administrators can define the start and end times for the trading day. Outside of these hours, the execution engine will reject all incoming orders, though users may still view their portfolios. Furthermore, a market calendar allows admins to designate specific dates as holidays or weekends, during which the entire exchange is paused to reflect real-world banking schedules.

3. Technical Design and Engineering Components

3.1 Stochastic Price Fluctuation Engine
At the heart of the simulation is the Random Stock Price Generator. To move beyond simple random walks, the engine utilizes a mathematical model that simulates intraday volatility. The price of an asset at time T is dependent on its price at time T-1, modified by a small, random percentage change. This ensures that price movements are fluid and continuous rather than erratic jumps. The engine is programmed to respect the volume constraints set by the administrator, ensuring that price increases do not violate the logic of supply and demand within the simulation.

3.2 Data Visualization and Frontend GUI
The web interface serves as the terminal for both customers and admins. It is designed to present dense financial data in a readable format. The market dashboard includes several calculated fields:

Market Capitalization Calculation:
The system dynamically calculates the market cap for every listed entity using the following formal relationship:
$$Market\ Capitalization = Total\ Volume \times Current\ Price$$

Historical Performance Data:
The GUI tracks the opening price of the day, as well as the daily high and daily low. These values are reset at the start of every new trading day as defined by the administrator's schedule.

3.3 Backend Relational Database Schema
The database is the source of truth for the entire application. It is structured into multiple tables to ensure data normalization.

User Table: Stores primary identifiers, encrypted passwords, and account status.
Stock Table: Stores ticker symbols, company names, current prices, and total volume.
Transaction Ledger: A high-volume table that records every buy and sell event, including the user ID, stock ID, price at execution, and an ISO 8601 timestamp.
Cash Event Log: Records all deposits and withdrawals to maintain a clear audit trail of user liquidity.
System Audit Table: Tracks administrative changes such as the creation of new stocks or modifications to market hours for security auditing.

4. Logical Architecture (3-Tier Structure)

The application follows a standard 3-tier architecture to ensure that the presentation, logic, and data storage layers remain independent.

Tier 1: The Presentation Tier
Located on the user's local machine or mobile device, this tier is composed of HTML5, CSS3, and JavaScript. It is served by the Flask application but executes within the client's browser environment. Its primary role is to capture user events and transmit them to the server via standardized HTTP protocols.

Tier 2: The Application Tier
Hosted on an Amazon EC2 instance within a public subnet, this tier acts as the intermediary. It is built using the Flask web framework and Python. All business logic, from the verification of funds to the calculation of market hours, happens here. It listens on Port 8000 and is the only component that can communicate with both the external internet and the internal database.

Tier 3: The Data Tier
The Data Tier is powered by a MySQL instance running on Amazon RDS. For maximum security, this tier is placed within a private subnet. It has no direct route to the internet. Access is restricted to Tier 2 via Port 3306. This isolation ensures that even if the web server is compromised, the core financial records remain shielded behind secondary network layers.

5. System Integrity and Scalability

Concurrency Handling:
In a multi-user environment, it is possible for two users to attempt to buy the last remaining shares of a stock simultaneously. The system implements database-level locking to ensure that transactions are processed sequentially, preventing the over-selling of assets.

Auditability:
Every action within the system is recorded with a timestamp. This allows administrators to reconstruct the state of the market at any given second. This feature is vital for training, as it allows instructors to review a student's trading strategy post-session.

Security Posture:
The use of private subnets and security groups ensures that the attack surface of the application is minimized. Sensitive data is never transmitted in plain text, and the separation of concerns between tiers prevents single points of failure from compromising the entire system's data integrity.

Future Extensibility:
The modular nature of the 3-tier design allows for future updates, such as the addition of limit orders, margin trading, or advanced technical analysis charts, without requiring a complete overhaul of the existing codebase.

6. Conclusion
The Stock Trading Training System is a robust solution for financial education. By combining a realistic trading engine with a secure cloud-based architecture, it provides a powerful platform for the next generation of financial professionals to hone their skills in a controlled, measurable environment.

7. System Maintenance and Operational Procedures
To ensure the long-term viability of the Stock Trading Training System, several operational protocols must be followed. These procedures cover system updates, data backups, and performance monitoring.

Daily Data Backups:
The Amazon RDS instance is configured to perform automated daily snapshots. These backups are stored across multiple availability zones to prevent data loss in the event of a regional outage. In the event of a catastrophic failure, the system can be restored to its last known healthy state within minutes.

Performance Monitoring and Logging:
The Application Tier utilizes CloudWatch logs to monitor CPU utilization and memory consumption. If the number of concurrent users exceeds the capacity of the single EC2 instance, the system is designed to be horizontally scalable. This means additional EC2 instances can be launched and placed behind a Load Balancer to distribute the traffic evenly.

Software Update Cycle:
Updates to the Python Flask application are managed through a Git-based workflow. Code changes are first pushed to a staging environment where they undergo automated testing. Once the tests pass, the code is deployed to the production EC2 instance. This prevents bugs in the price generator or trading logic from reaching the end users.

Administrative Oversight:
Administrators are encouraged to review the System Audit Table weekly. This review helps identify any unusual patterns, such as a high volume of cancelled orders or unexpected price swings, which might indicate a need to tune the random price generator's volatility parameters.

8. Summary of User Roles and Permissions
Access control is enforced at the Application Tier to ensure users can only perform actions relevant to their roles.

Customer Permissions:
Can view public market data.
Can manage their own profile and cash account.
Can execute trades for stocks they can afford.
Cannot access administrative panels or modify stock parameters.

Administrator Permissions:
Full access to stock creation and management.
Ability to override market hours and schedules.
Access to global transaction logs for monitoring.
Cannot execute trades using customer accounts, maintaining a separation between market makers and market participants.

This structure ensures a transparent and fair simulation environment for all participants.

9. Deployment Configuration
The deployment utilizes an Infrastructure as Code (IaC) approach where possible. The VPC (Virtual Private Cloud) is configured with two public subnets for the Application Tier and two private subnets for the Data Tier. This redundancy ensures that if one data center goes offline, the system remains accessible. Security groups are configured with the principle of least privilege, allowing only necessary traffic on Ports 8000 and 3306.

By following this comprehensive documentation, the IT department can ensure the Stock Trading Training System remains a reliable and effective tool for the company's training needs.
